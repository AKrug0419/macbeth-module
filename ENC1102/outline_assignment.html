📝 Assignment Instructions

Create an outline that identifies which topic you're writing about and maps out the topic and major ideas of each body paragraph. Step 1
Choose your topic

Select one of the three available topics from the Prewriting & Planning page. The topics are based on the three short stories you've analyzed this semester: Hurston's "Sweat," Carter's "The Company of Wolves," and Roth's "The Conversion of the Jews." Each topic focuses on a central theme and is organized around the same analytical moves you practiced in your study question assignments.

Step 2
Review the sample outline

Before writing your outline, review the sample research essay outline. It models the level of detail and structure expected in your submission — including how to break a thematic topic into distinct body paragraph arguments and how to plan your primary and secondary source evidence at each stage.

Step 3
Write your outline

Create your own outline based on the topic and story you selected. Follow this structure:

I. Introduction Paragraph Identify your topic — for example, "The Theme of Domestic Violence and Symbolism in Hurston's 'Sweat.'" You do not need to write the full introduction here.
II–IV. Body Paragraphs (3–4) For each body paragraph, identify the paragraph's topic and one to two key ideas. Use sub-points (A., B.) and details (1., 2.) to show what each paragraph will cover, including planned evidence from the story and your scholarly sources.
V. Conclusion Paragraph You can leave this blank, just as the sample outline does. Conclusions are easier to write once the body paragraphs are drafted.

📋 How This Is Graded

There is no rubric for this assignment. Your outline will be reviewed to ensure that the paragraph topics and ideas are clear, narrow, and relevant to the overall essay topic you selected.

💡 Formative Assessment

This is a formative assessment: as long as you complete the assignment, you'll receive full credit. If there are any concerns with your outline, feedback will be noted directly on your submission when it is assessed.

⚖️ Academic Integrity

Valencia College is dedicated to maintaining academic standards grounded in transparency, integrity, and creating the right conditions for learning. All work submitted must reflect your own original reading, thinking, and writing. Valencia Policy 6Hx28:8-11: Academic Dishonesty ↗

Original Work

All submitted work must be your own. Your outline should reflect your own reading, thinking, and planning — not AI-generated content or another student's ideas.

Consequences

Violations may result in a zero for this assignment. Students have one week to respond to any allegation of academic dishonesty before a penalty is assigned.

🤖 AI & Technology Policy for This Assignment
✅ Grammarly — Permitted (with limits)

You may use Grammarly to identify grammar, punctuation, and sentence errors only. Use the "Correction" option only. Do not use "Clarity," "Engagement," or "Delivery" — these rewrite your sentences and constitute a violation.

❌ AI Generators — Not Permitted

Do not use ChatGPT, Gemini, or similar tools to generate your outline or any part of it. This violates the academic integrity policy.

❌ Online Translators — Not Permitted

Do not use online translators to translate your outline into English. Translated text is no longer your own writing and constitutes a violation of academic integrity.

🔍 Canvas AI Report

Your instructor has access to Canvas's AI Report feature (separate from Turnitin). If your submission generates a flag, you will be asked to redo the assignment — in part or in whole — depending on what the report identifies.
