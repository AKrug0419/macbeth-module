Pause Point 01 of 06
The Opening Arrest & Agatha's Vision
Technological Control
Scene still · Pause Point 01 · ~0:08

What just happened: We have seen PreCrime in action for the first time. A man is about to murder his wife and her lover; Anderton's team reads the precogs' vision and arrests him before the act occurs. The man is confused, disoriented — he doesn't understand why he's been arrested, because from his perspective, he hasn't done anything. The precogs are shown briefly: three human beings floating in a tank, their neural activity harvested as raw data.

Pause Activity 01 Identify the Characteristic

The PreCrime system we've just seen in action illustrates multiple characteristics of a dystopian society. Which characteristic is most directly and powerfully demonstrated by the arrest itself — not the technology, not the precogs, but the act of detaining someone for a crime they have not yet committed?

Pause Point 02 of 06
The Eye Wall & Personalized Advertising
Corporate Control

What just happened: Anderton walks through a public mall. Retinal scanners on every surface identify him by eye and serve him personalized advertisements by name — for products tailored to his purchase history, emotional profile, and current location. The ads speak directly to him. No one around him finds this remarkable.

Pause Activity 02 Identify the Control Type

The personalized advertising sequence combines two types of dystopian control working simultaneously. Which pairing best describes what we've just seen?

Pause Point 03 of 06
The Hall of Souls
Dehumanization
Scene still · Pause Point 03 · ~0:58

What just happened: We've seen PreCrime's containment facility — called the "Hall of Souls" — where those convicted of pre-murder are stored indefinitely in suspended animation, sealed in individual cocoons, stacked in rows. They are not imprisoned in any conventional sense; they are warehoused. Alive, but not living. Convicted of nothing they actually did.

Pause Activity 03 Identify the Characteristic

The Hall of Souls is one of the film's most disturbing images. Which characteristic of a dystopian society does it most directly illustrate?

Anderton's Own PreCrime Card
Protagonist Trait

What just happened: The precogs have just named Anderton himself as a future murderer. His own system — the one he built, the one he has defended and believed in — has turned on him. He is now the target of the machinery he designed. He has 36 hours before his own arrest team comes for him.

Pause Activity 04 Identify the Protagonist Trait

This is the turning point for Anderton as the film's dystopian protagonist. Which of the four protagonist traits becomes undeniable the moment he sees his own name on that card?

Pause Point 05 of 06
Eye Replacement & The Spider-Bots
Surveillance
Scene still · Pause Point 05 · ~1:35

What just happened: To evade the retinal scanners used by every surface in the city, Anderton has undergone illegal eye-replacement surgery — replacing his own eyes to assume a new identity. Meanwhile, spider-bot drones have flooded his building, scanning every resident's retinas through doors, walls, and eyelids. The city is a total surveillance environment; the only way to escape it is to change who you biologically are.

Pause Activity 05 Identify the Characteristic

Anderton's eye replacement and the spider-bot sequence together illustrate a characteristic taken to its absolute extreme. The surveillance system in this scene is so total that escape requires changing your own body. Which characteristic does this most powerfully illustrate?

Pause Point 06 of 06
Burgess Revealed & The Ending
Illusion of Utopia

What just happened: Director Lamar Burgess — PreCrime's founder and Anderton's mentor — has been revealed as the system's original criminal. He committed a murder to protect PreCrime in its infancy, then suppressed the minority report that would have exposed it. The system built to prevent murder was founded on one. Faced with Anderton's trap, Burgess makes a choice — and PreCrime is shut down. The precogs are freed.

Pause Activity 06 Identify the Characteristic

Burgess's revelation — that the man who built the perfect crime-prevention system committed the very crime it was designed to prevent — is the film's definitive dystopian statement. Which characteristic of a dystopian society does the entire arc of the film, culminating here, most powerfully illustrate?
