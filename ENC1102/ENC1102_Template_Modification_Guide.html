ENC 1102 Course Template
Modification Guide

Hello!

Welcome to the School of Writing & Literature's ENC1102 Course Template.

Initially the product of a collaborative work-team originating on the East Campus, including a review-and-revision process involving all full and part-time faculty, the Template is now available to all faculty in the School of Writing and Literature.

Created originally during the Pandemic to fulfill the college's request for a self-contained, outcomes-based resource for faculty new to online learning, it has since undergone several revisions. Each time, however, two core design principles informed the work: First, pedagogical alignment with Valencia's online learning outcomes and best practices and, second, a commitment to ensuring that faculty would be able to adapt core elements of the Template to match their individual teaching philosophies.

To satisfy both objectives, some elements of the template are open to modification while others must remain fixed.

Below, please find a detailed explanation of what instructors currently can and cannot change in the Template. These modification rules are open to change, of course, based on the input of the SOWL's administrative team in collaboration with all faculty stakeholders.

Permitted Modifications

Permitted
01

Orientation Module Assignment

The assignment included in the Orientation Module may be replaced or revised to reflect an instructor's preferred approach to course introduction. Instructors choosing to modify this assignment are responsible for ensuring the replacement remains appropriate to the orientation context and serves a comparable preparatory function for students entering the course.

02

Specific Literary Selections

Instructors may replace the short stories and poems currently in the template with works of their own choosing. However, replacements must meet all three of the following conditions:

Conditions for Replacement

  1. Legal and Free Access. The work must be available to students at no cost and through a legally permitted source (a college database, college ebook platform, or the Valencia Reader). Works that are not legally accessible in one of these formats may not be used.
  2. Sufficient Critical Scholarship. Because students are required to locate and use secondary source literary criticism in their research essays, any replacement work must be critically significant enough to have generated substantive scholarship. Instructors are strongly advised to search the college's literature databases and ebook collections to confirm that adequate secondary sources exist before finalizing a selection. Note that sources focused primarily on the author's biography or the historical period will not serve the research assignment's requirements; the scholarship must engage directly with the literary work itself.
  3. Instructor-Created Study Questions and Essay Topics. Instructors are responsible for developing all accompanying study questions and essay topics for any literary work they introduce. Existing questions and prompts in the template are written for the current selections and will not transfer to replacement works.
03

Rubrics

The grading rubrics provided in the template may be modified to reflect an instructor's preferred evaluative criteria and point distributions. Instructors revising rubrics should ensure that the revised criteria remain consistent with the assignment's stated purpose and the relevant course outcomes.

04

Learning Materials

The learning materials already in place in the template (instructional pages, concept explanations, guides, and examples) may not be deleted or substantively altered. Minor proofreading corrections are acceptable. Instructors are welcome, however, to supplement the existing materials with additional resources of their own: supplementary readings, videos, external links, or instructional pages they have developed.

05

AI Policy

The template includes a default AI use policy consistent with college guidelines. Instructors may update or replace this policy to reflect their own approach and teaching philosophy regarding student use of AI tools, provided the revised policy does not conflict with any applicable college or department policy on academic integrity.

NOTE: The template currently permits the limited use of Grammarly's "Correction" function, which highlights grammar and punctuation errors. It forbids, however, the use of Grammarly's other three functions: "Clarity," "Engagement," and "Delivery," all of which revise student language, phrases, and sentences. In addition, the template informs students that any assignment that generates a Canvas AI Report will have to be re-done and resubmitted. Rarely, a student will deny having used Grammarly or similar tools (online translators, large language model AI systems). In that case, instructors will have to have a conversation with the student to determine the most appropriate next steps.

Instructors are not required to allow students to use Grammarly or any other grammar-assistance program, however. Please make your specific policy transparent for students, though, to minimize future conflicts. Also, instructors will need to edit course assignments so that their own policy is clearly stated.

06

Late Assignment & Grace Period Policy

The template currently employs a grace period structure for all assignments, designed in alignment with the college's "Cruelty Free Syllabus" professional development framework. This approach is intended to reduce the frequency of grade-dispute conflicts (one of the most common sources of student-instructor tension reported by the college) by providing students with a structured window before penalty applies.

Essays

4-day grace period before the late policy applies

All Other Assignments

2-day grace period before the late policy applies

Late Penalty: Non-Essay

2 points deducted per 24-hour period after grace period

Late Penalty: Essays

5 points deducted per 24-hour period after grace period

Both the grace period durations and the specific late penalty deductions may be changed to reflect an instructor's preferred policy. Instructors who modify this section are responsible for updating all relevant syllabus and assignment language to reflect their chosen approach.

Instructors are not required to use the grace period system, however. Moreover, instructors can devise their own late policies based on their personal teaching philosophy.

07

Final Exam Content

The Final Exam is currently a multiple-choice, auto-graded assessment focused exclusively on MLA documentation skills and plagiarism awareness. Literary concepts are not assessed on the final exam. This format was chosen to minimize instructor grading workload at the end of the semester while still measuring skills directly tied to the course's principal outcomes: skills students will most likely carry into future academic work.

Instructors may revise the content of the exam's individual questions. However, two constraints apply:

Constraints

The multiple-choice, auto-graded format may not be changed. Additionally, any revised questions must demonstrably address specific course outcomes. Instructors who modify exam questions are responsible for verifying this alignment before publishing.

Fixed Course Elements

Not Modifiable
1

Course Outcomes

The course outcomes are set at the department and college level and may not be altered, reordered, or removed. All course activities and assessments must remain oriented toward these outcomes.

2

Overall Course Organization

The template's module structure, sequencing, and overall organizational logic may not be changed. This includes the order and grouping of modules, the placement of major assessments within the arc of the course, and the relationship between learning materials and assignments.

3

Department & College Policies

All policies mandated at the department or college level (including academic integrity policies, accessibility requirements, and any other institutionally required language) must remain in place and unaltered.

4

Essay Requirements

The requirements governing the three major essays (including minimum lengths, primary and secondary source integration requirements, and secondary source requirements) may not be changed.

5

Essay Genres

All three major essays in the template require students to critically analyze literary or cinematic texts through a traditional academic essay format. Please do not convert these to personal narratives, reflection papers, creative writing assignments, or other non-analytical genres that deviate from the introduction, body, and conclusion standard of academic writing.
