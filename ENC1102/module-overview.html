About This Module

This module is devoted to studying a classic literary genre: dystopian literature. We'll explore this popular style of narrative by viewing and then analyzing a film — either The Matrix, available via one of the college's databases, or a movie or TV show of your own choosing.

What Is Dystopian Literature?

Dystopian narratives imagine societies that have gone wrong — often catastrophically so. Where utopian fiction dreams of a perfect world, dystopian fiction exposes how easily the pursuit of perfection produces oppression. Citizens are controlled, individuality is suppressed, and the truth is hidden. The genre has been one of the most powerful vehicles for social criticism in the history of both literature and film.
