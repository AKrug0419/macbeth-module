SEC · 01

What the Works Cited Is, and Why It Is Annoying

Prepare to be annoyed.

Creating a Works Cited for a research essay is frustrating. Worse, teachers are allowed to make it worth as much of the essay's grade as they want, so too many errors on it can make your grade cry.

The Works Cited serves a very important function: it provides all of the bibliographic information about every source cited inside your essay, both your primary source (the film) and your secondary sources. Fortunately, a significant portion of the tedious work was already done for you during the Source Log Assignment, where you used each database's Citation Tool to collect bibliographic information for your secondary sources in MLA Works Cited format.

The main challenge is not the entries themselves. It is that formatting errors almost always appear when you transfer those entries from the Source Log into your essay document. Spacing shifts, indentations get lost, URLs become hyperlinks, and punctuation disappears. The problems section below walks through exactly how to recognize and fix each issue. Open your completed Source Log Assignment before you get started.

SEC · 02

What Goes Wrong When You Transfer Entries, and How to Fix It

When you copy and paste your Works Cited entries from the Source Log into your essay document, the transfer almost never comes through cleanly. Below are the six most common problems to check for after pasting.

Problem 1 :: Hanging Indent Missing

MLA Works Cited entries use a hanging indent: the first line of each entry starts at the left margin, and all subsequent lines are indented half an inch. This formatting is almost always lost when you paste entries in.

Looks wrong: All lines flush to the left margin with no indentation at all.
Fix: Select the entry, open the paragraph formatting menu, and set a hanging indent of 0.5 inch. The first video below demonstrates this step by step.
Problem 2 :: Double Spacing Lost or Added

The Works Cited page must be double spaced throughout, both within each entry and between entries. No extra blank lines between entries, and no single spaced lines within them.

Looks wrong: Single spaced entries, or large gaps where an extra blank line was inserted between them.
Fix: Select all text on the Works Cited page and apply double spacing. Delete any blank lines between entries.
Problem 3 :: URLs Are Hyperlinked

When a URL is pasted into a Word document, it automatically becomes a clickable blue hyperlink. MLA format requires URLs to appear as plain text, not styled hyperlinks.

Looks wrong: URL appears in blue underlined text.
Fix: Right click the link and select "Remove Hyperlink." Then check that the text color matches the rest of the entry. The second video below covers this in detail.
Problem 4 :: Long URLs Breaking Awkwardly

Long web addresses sometimes push a line so far to the right that it creates a large blank space in the middle of the entry, making it look incorrectly formatted.

Looks wrong: A line that ends after one or two words with a large gap before the URL continues on the next line.
Fix: The second video below demonstrates the specific technique for handling long URL line breaks.
Problem 5 :: Font or Size Mismatch

Pasted text often brings its own font and size. Your Works Cited entries may end up in a different typeface or size than the rest of your essay, a clear formatting violation.

Looks wrong: Works Cited entries appear in a slightly different font, size, or weight than the essay body.
Fix: Select all the entries and apply your essay's standard font and size, Times New Roman 12 point for MLA.
Problem 6 :: Citation Tool Errors

Database Citation Tools are good but not perfect. They occasionally generate entries with missing punctuation, incorrect capitalization, or the wrong format for a specific source type.

Looks wrong: A missing period at the end of an entry, an all caps title, or an obviously blank or placeholder field.
Fix: Compare each entry against the handout's models and correct any discrepancies by hand.

SEC · 04

Works Cited Entry for Your Film

Your primary source, the film you are writing about, also needs to appear on your Works Cited page. Unlike your secondary sources, no Citation Tool will generate this entry for you.

Which Films Are on the Approved List

Your essay will focus on Alfonso Cuarón's Children of Men (2006), Guillermo del Toro's Pan's Labyrinth (2006), or the Wachowskis' The Matrix (1999). When you build your Works Cited entry, use the correct director, production companies, and year for whichever film you chose. The handout below covers the standard MLA 9 format for a feature film.

Use the handout linked below to format the Works Cited entry for your film. It walks through the correct MLA format for a feature length film, field by field: title in italics, director, key performers, production companies, and year.

Because the film has no single author at the front of the entry, place it alphabetically by the first word of its title, ignoring "A," "An," and "The." Children of Men alphabetizes under C, Pan's Labyrinth under P, and The Matrix under M (ignoring "The").

A complete Works Cited for this essay will have four entries: one for your film and one for each of your three secondary sources.

The entries must appear in alphabetical order by the author's last name. If a source has no individual author listed (the film itself, or a website page attributed only to an organization), alphabetize by the first word of the title, ignoring articles like "A," "An," and "The."

Film Specific Note

Feature film titles go in italics. Episode titles and short film titles use quotation marks, but feature length film titles do not. Production company names (Universal Pictures, Warner Bros., Telecinco Cinema) are not italicized when they appear in the publisher position of the entry.

SEC · 05

Final Works Cited Checklist

Run through each item below before submitting your essay. Every checked box is a formatting error you will not lose points for.

✅ Works Cited :: Final Check
Titled Works Cited, centered at the top, not bolded, not in quotation marks
Entries in alphabetical order by author's last name, or by title for the film
Entire page is double spaced with no extra blank lines between entries
Hanging indent applied to all entries (first line flush left, subsequent lines indented 0.5 inch)
All URLs are plain text with no blue underlined hyperlinks
Font and size match the rest of the essay (Times New Roman 12 point)
Four entries total: your film plus three secondary sources
Every source cited in the essay appears on the Works Cited, and vice versa
