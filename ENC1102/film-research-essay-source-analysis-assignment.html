SEC · 02

Two Parts, Three Sources

For your Dystopian Film Research Essay, you will be working with three secondary sources. You will locate them using two different methods: one through an academic database, and two through internet research, using the search strategies and evaluation tools you studied in this module.

This assignment has two parts. In Part One, you will submit MLA formatted citations for all three sources. In Part Two, you will write a brief SIFT based evaluation for each of your two internet sources, demonstrating how you verified them before deciding to use them.

A Note on Source Types

All three sources should be film criticism or film scholarship. These are secondary sources that analyze, interpret, or discuss the film (or the filmmaker's work) you are writing about. Your sources should be written by scholars, critics, or journalists with relevant expertise, published in academic or established critical contexts, and directly relevant to your essay's argument about Children of Men, Pan's Labyrinth, or The Matrix.

Part Two
SIFT Evaluation of Your Internet Sources

For each of your two internet sources, write a brief evaluation using the four prompts below. Work through each prompt in order, labeling your response for each one. Your evaluation of each source should be at least 150 words.

You are demonstrating that you actually investigated these sources before deciding to use them, not just that they look credible at first glance. Your evaluations should be specific: name the websites, publications, or search results you found during your lateral investigation, not just general impressions.

Answer Each of the Following for Both Internet Sources
  • S
    Stop · How You Found It
    What search engine and search terms did you use to find this source? What was your initial impression of it, and what made you decide it was worth investigating further?
    If you used an AI tool to generate search keywords, briefly describe how you used it and what terms it suggested.
  • I
    Investigate · Your Lateral Research
    What did you find when you left the page and searched for the source, publication, or author in a new browser tab? Who publishes this source, and what did other sources tell you about its credibility?
    Be specific: name what you searched for and what you found. "It looked legitimate" is not a sufficient answer here.
  • F
    Find · Corroboration
    What other credible sources corroborate this source's focus or arguments? Does the scholarly conversation about your film support the general direction this source takes?
    You do not need sources that make exactly the same argument, just evidence that the critical conversation this source participates in is real and established.
  • T
    Trace · Credibility Conclusion
    Based on your investigation, what specific evidence makes you confident this source is credible and appropriate for an academic research essay? What would have disqualified it?
    Consider: author credentials, publisher reputation, citation practices within the source, and whether any claims seemed unverifiable.

Part Two · SIFT Evaluations Complete a SIFT evaluation for each internet source

For each source, all four SIFT letters are addressed in order. Responses name specific search terms, websites, and publications, not just general impressions.

Internet Source One (Source 2) · SIFT Evaluation
S
Stop What search engine and search terms did you use? What was your first impression?
If you used an AI tool to suggest keywords, briefly describe how and what terms it offered.
I searched Google using the terms Children of Men Cuarón long take camera scholarly analysis. The first page returned a mix of fan blogs, news features, and one result from a site called Senses of Cinema. The URL ended in .com but the page title described a feature article on focalisation and narrative asymmetry in the film, which sounded considerably more analytical than the surrounding results. The article had a named author, footnotes, and a list of works cited at the bottom, which made me decide it was worth investigating further.
I
Investigate What did you find when you left the page and searched the source or author in a new tab?
Name the specific websites, publications, or results you found. "It looked legitimate" is not sufficient.
I opened a new tab and searched Senses of Cinema journal academic peer reviewed. I learned that Senses of Cinema is an independent, online quarterly film journal founded in 1999 in Australia, with an international editorial board and an advisory board that includes recognized film scholars. The Wikipedia entry on the journal cited its inclusion in academic film studies course syllabi and described its editorial review process. I then searched for Ben Ogrodnik and found that he has published peer reviewed work on film and media in venues including Film Criticism and has been listed as a graduate scholar at the University of Pittsburgh. His other Senses of Cinema essays appeared on the same site and dealt with similar topics in narrative film theory.
F
Find What other credible sources corroborate this source's focus or arguments?
You do not need exact agreement, just evidence the critical conversation it joins is real and established.
My JSTOR search for Children of Men long take Cuarón returned several articles discussing the film's camera technique and political critique, including the Samuel Amago article in Discourse that I am using as my database source. Both the Amago and the Ogrodnik essays foreground the relationship between camera movement and political meaning in the film, even though they reach somewhat different conclusions about how the spectator is positioned. The critical conversation Ogrodnik is participating in is clearly active and ongoing.
T
Trace What specific evidence makes you confident this source is credible for academic use?
Consider: author credentials, publisher reputation, citation practices, and any unverifiable claims.
I am confident this source is appropriate for academic use for three reasons: Senses of Cinema is an established film studies journal with an editorial board and a long publication history, the author has a verifiable graduate research profile and other peer reviewed work on film, and the article itself uses footnotes and an in text bibliography rather than unsupported assertions. What would have disqualified it: an anonymous author, a site with no editorial structure, or an article that just summarized the plot of the film without making an interpretive argument. None of those problems were present.
Internet Source Two (Source 3) · SIFT Evaluation
S
Stop What search engine and search terms did you use? What was your first impression?
If you used an AI tool to suggest keywords, briefly describe how and what terms it offered.
I used Google with the search terms Children of Men contemporary politics dystopia essay 2020. I asked an AI assistant for additional keywords and it suggested adding capitalist realism and refugee crisis, which narrowed my results. The article in New Statesman by Gavin Jacobson appeared on the first page. The headline argued that the film "haunts the present moment," which suggested a sustained interpretive argument rather than a list of trivia, and the byline identified a named writer, so it seemed worth a closer look.
I
Investigate What did you find when you left the page and searched the source or author in a new tab?
Name the specific websites, publications, or results you found. "It looked legitimate" is not sufficient.
In a new tab, I searched New Statesman magazine credibility and read its Wikipedia entry, which described it as a long established British weekly founded in 1913, known for political and cultural commentary. I then searched Gavin Jacobson writer and found his author page at New Statesman, where he is listed as a Senior Writer specializing in ideas and politics, with a substantial archive of essays. His byline also appeared on book reviews for The New York Review of Books and Foreign Affairs. None of the results I found suggested he was anonymous, undisclosed, or unaffiliated with established publications.
F
Find What other credible sources corroborate this source's focus or arguments?
You do not need exact agreement, just evidence the critical conversation it joins is real and established.
A search in JSTOR and Academic Search Complete for criticism that links Children of Men to contemporary politics returned multiple peer reviewed articles discussing the film's relevance to refugee policy, surveillance, and what theorists have called "capitalist realism," the same framework Jacobson invokes. Multiple academic essays cite Mark Fisher's book Capitalist Realism in connection with Cuarón's film, which is the central reference Jacobson is drawing on. Jacobson's argument fits squarely within an established critical conversation rather than standing alone.
T
Trace What specific evidence makes you confident this source is credible for academic use?
Consider: author credentials, publisher reputation, citation practices, and any unverifiable claims.
The combination of a long established publication with an editorial process, a named senior writer with a verifiable byline at multiple reputable outlets, and an argument that draws on a recognizable theoretical framework makes me confident this source is appropriate for academic use. It is a journalistic essay rather than a peer reviewed scholarly article, so I plan to use it for context and argumentative framing rather than as my primary scholarly authority. What would have disqualified it: an unsigned post, a site with no editorial oversight, or claims about the film that I could not corroborate elsewhere. None of those problems were present.

This assignment will be assessed on the accuracy of your MLA citations, the completeness of all three source entries in Part One, and the specificity and depth of your SIFT evaluations in Part Two. Evaluations that offer only general impressions without naming specific evidence from your lateral investigation will not receive full credit. There is no separate rubric for this assignment.
