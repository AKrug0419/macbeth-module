Module Seven  ·  ENC 1102

Poetry Research Essay

Finding Sources · Evaluating Credibility · Writing the Analysis

Module Overview

What We're Doing This Module

This module is devoted to writing your third essay for ENC 1102: a brief, documented research essay analyzing the use of poetic imagery and/or figurative language in one of the six poems from the previous module.

✦
Goals

Course Learning Outcomes & Module Objectives

This module addresses the following course-level learning outcomes. By the end you'll be able to meet the specific objectives listed on the right.

Course Learning Outcomes
  • Apply conventions aligned to varied writing situations.
  • Document source material in accordance with at least one recognized documentation style.
  • Apply critical reading skills.
By the End of This Module
  • Use academic databases to locate a relevant source for a research essay.
  • Use the internet to locate a credible source for a research essay.
  • Evaluate the credibility of an internet source.
  • Use a database Citation Tool to obtain bibliographic information.
  • Plan, write, edit, and proofread an essay with an introduction, body paragraphs, conclusion, and Works Cited.
  • Integrate quotes from primary and secondary sources using the MLA documentation system.
  • Analyze a poem's use of poetic imagery and/or figurative language.

Module Pages

What's Covered in This Module

Work through the pages below in order. Each stage of the writing process has its own dedicated page.

1
The Writing Process: Prewriting and Planning
Choosing a poem and topic, reviewing your prior analysis, and outlining your essay.
2
Using the Internet for Academic Research
How to search strategically and evaluate websites for credibility, with criteria and worked examples.
3
Using AI for Academic Research
Why AI generated content cannot serve as a secondary source, and how to use AI responsibly as a research aid.
4
Poetry Research Essay Source Analysis Assignment
Locate one database source and two internet sources, evaluate each for credibility, and submit a brief analysis of all three.
5
MLA Documentation: How to Write a Summary
Condensing a source's argument into a brief, accurately attributed overview.
6
The Writing Process: Drafting and Revising
Writing a complete draft with a fully annotated sample essay and a revising checklist.
7
The Writing Process: Editing and Proofreading
Sentence level editing, source level auditing, and final proofreading for grammar and citations.
8
Poetry and Literary Elements Research Essay
The full 700 word minimum research essay with a Works Cited page integrating three secondary sources.

2
Poetry & Literary Elements Research Essay
Due Sunday, April 26th at 11:59 p.m.  ·  No grace period
