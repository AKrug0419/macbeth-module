Building, formatting, and cleaning up your Works Cited entries

📄 The Works Cited

Prepare to be annoyed.

Creating a Works Cited for a research essay is frustrating. Worse, teachers are allowed to make the Works Cited worth as much of an essay's grade as they want — so having too many errors on it can make your grade cry.

The Works Cited serves a very important function: it provides all of the bibliographic information about every source cited inside your essay — both your primary source and your secondary sources. Fortunately, quite a bit of the tedious part was already done for you during the Research Assignment.

💡 You've Already Done Part of This

When you completed the Research Assignment, you used each database's Citation Tool to collect the bibliographic information for your three secondary sources in MLA Works Cited format.

Open that completed assignment now — you'll need it to follow along with the steps handout below.

The main challenge isn't the entries themselves — it's that formatting errors almost always appear when you transfer those entries from the Research Assignment into your essay document. Spacing shifts, indentations get lost, URLs become hyperlinks, and punctuation disappears. The handout available on the course page walks through exactly how to fix these problems step by step.

🔎 What Goes Wrong When You Transfer Entries — and How to Fix It

When you copy and paste your Works Cited entries from the Research Assignment into your essay document, the transfer almost never comes through cleanly. Below are the six most common problems students encounter — check your entries against each one after pasting:

Problem 1 — Hanging Indent Missing

MLA Works Cited entries use a hanging indent: the first line starts at the left margin, and all subsequent lines are indented half an inch. When you paste entries in, this formatting is usually lost.

Wrong: All lines flush to the left margin.
Fix: Select the entry, open the paragraph formatting menu, and set a hanging indent of 0.5". Video 1 below demonstrates this.
Problem 2 — Double Spacing Lost or Added

The Works Cited page must be double-spaced throughout — both within each entry and between entries. No extra blank lines between entries.

Wrong: Single-spaced entries, or large gaps between them from an extra blank line.
Fix: Select all text on the Works Cited page and apply double spacing. Delete any blank lines between entries.
Problem 3 — URLs Are Hyperlinked

When a URL is pasted into Word, it automatically becomes a clickable blue hyperlink. MLA format requires URLs to appear as plain black text.

Wrong: URL appears in blue underlined text.
Fix: Right-click the link and select "Remove Hyperlink." Video 2 below covers URL formatting in detail.
Problem 4 — Long URLs Breaking Awkwardly

Long web addresses sometimes push a line so far to the right that it creates a large blank space in the middle of the entry.

Wrong: A line that ends after one or two words with a large gap before the URL continues.
Fix: Video 2 below demonstrates the specific technique for handling this.
Problem 5 — Font or Size Mismatch

Pasted text often brings its own font and size along with it. Your Works Cited entries may end up in a different typeface or size than the rest of your essay.

Wrong: Works Cited entries appear in a slightly different font or size than the essay body.
Fix: Select all the entries and apply your essay's standard font and size (Times New Roman 12pt for MLA).
Problem 6 — Citation Tool Errors

Database Citation Tools are good but not perfect. They occasionally generate entries with missing punctuation, incorrect capitalization, or the wrong format.

Wrong: A missing period at the end, all-caps title, or a blank/placeholder field.
Fix: Compare each entry against the handout's models and correct any discrepancies by hand.

✅ Final Works Cited Checklist — Before You Submit Your Essay
Run through each item before submitting:
☐   Titled Works Cited — centered, not bolded, not in quotes
☐   All URLs are plain text — no blue hyperlinks
☐   Entries in alphabetical order by author's last name
☐   Font and size match the rest of the essay
☐   Entire page is double-spaced — no extra blank lines
☐   Four entries total: one primary source + three secondary sources
☐   Hanging indent applied to all entries
☐   Every source cited in the essay appears on the Works Cited — and vice versa
